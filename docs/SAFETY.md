# Safety Considerations

## Professional Administrative Context

Grey Rock operates as a professional Executive Assistant utilizing the Grey Rock communications protocol — the same role performed by executive assistants, chiefs of staff, and communications managers who handle correspondence for principals across business, government, and legal contexts. The system follows the Grey Rock / BIFF protocol for managing high-conflict correspondence.

## When to Stop Grey Rocking

Grey rocking is a **communication management technique** with limits. Discontinue immediately if:

### Physical Safety Threats
- Direct threats of violence
- Indirect threats ("Something bad will happen", "You don't know what I'm capable of")
- Property destruction or stalking behavior

**Action**: Contact law enforcement. Consult a licensed attorney. An Executive Assistant communication system does not protect against physical danger.

### Extinction Bursts

When a high-conflict individual stops receiving emotional responses, behavior may temporarily escalate. This is called an extinction burst. Signs:

- Dramatic increase in message volume
- Escalating severity of language
- Threats to involve authorities, regulatory bodies, or media
- Sudden warmth or affection (love-bombing)
- Recruiting others to apply pressure
- Time-of-day shifts (messages at unusual hours)

Escalation timelines vary significantly by relationship history and individual. Consult appropriate professional counsel for guidance specific to your situation.

## Escalation Review Gates

The following topics are escalated to the principal (you) for direct handling. The Executive Assistant does not handle these autonomously:

- Sensitive legal, financial, or regulatory matters
- Violence or threats
- Stalking or harassment
- Financial claims or commitments
- Legal threats or references to attorneys
- Any communication the system is uncertain about

## Professional Escalation Framework

When situations exceed the scope of the Executive Assistant protocol, escalate appropriately:

| Situation | Escalation Path |
|---|---|
| Legal threats or litigation risk | Escalate to legal counsel |
| Workplace harassment or hostile behavior | Escalate to HR or compliance |
| Regulatory or compliance concerns | Escalate to compliance department |
| Physical threats or safety concerns | Escalate to law enforcement |
| Financial disputes exceeding threshold | Escalate to legal counsel or finance |
| Contractual violations | Escalate to legal counsel |

## Risks of Grey Rocking

Grey rocking is not without risk. The following matrix documents known risks with mitigation strategies:

| Risk | Description | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Extinction burst escalation** | Counterparty escalates dramatically when emotional supply is removed | High (expected behavior) | Anticipate per extinction schedule. Document everything. Consult counsel if threats emerge. |
| **Perceived contempt** | Neutral responses may be interpreted as dismissive or contemptuous | Moderate | Calibrate "friendly" carefully. Use EAR statements for Level 4. Avoid single-word responses in sensitive contexts. |
| **Over-depersonalization** | Excessive grey rocking in cooperative contexts damages productive relationships | Moderate | Use Grey Rock Cooperative mode for ongoing relationships. Reserve Strict mode for adversarial contacts. |
| **Legal misinterpretation** | Court may view minimal responses as non-cooperation or evasion | Low-Moderate | Ensure responses address all logistical content. Never ignore legitimate business requests. Consult attorney. |
| **Counterparty documentation** | Counterparty may use your grey rock responses as evidence of disengagement | Low-Moderate | Judge test every response. Maintain professional engagement floor for legitimate matters. |
| **Escalation to third parties** | Counterparty recruits allies, attorneys, or authorities in response to perceived stonewalling | Moderate | Respond to all messages. Address logistics thoroughly. Do not give ammunition for "they won't engage" narrative. |
| **Cultural misread** | Communication norms vary by culture; neutral tone may offend in some contexts | Moderate | Review Cultural Considerations below. Adjust calibration for context. |
| **AI detection** | Counterparty detects AI-assisted communication patterns | Low | Vary response structure. Use communication style training. Flag AI involvement questions for principal review. |

### Cultural Considerations

Communication norms vary significantly across cultures, industries, and professional contexts. Grey Rock's "neutral" tone may not read as neutral in all settings:

- **High-context cultures**: Minimal responses may be interpreted as hostile or disrespectful. Consider slightly warmer logistics handling (Grey Rock Cooperative mode).
- **Formal business cultures**: Executive style may be expected as baseline. Personal style grey rocking may read as dismissive.
- **Legal contexts**: Standards vary by jurisdiction. Some jurisdictions expect detailed, good-faith responses to all communications. Consult local counsel.
- **Industry norms**: Tech startup culture, legal practice, finance, and government each have different communication baselines. Calibrate accordingly.

The protocol's defaults are calibrated for professional English-language business communication in common-law jurisdictions. Users in other contexts should adjust calibration with appropriate professional guidance.

## Ethical Boundaries

### This Is Defensive, Not Offensive

Grey rocking is a communication management technique for high-conflict situations. It must NEVER be used to:
- Punish or manipulate a cooperative counterparty
- Avoid legitimate communication responsibilities
- Evade legal obligations
- Stonewall someone communicating in good faith

### If You Are the High-Conflict One

This protocol assumes defensive use. If you recognize high-conflict patterns in your own behavior, seek professional help before deploying communication management strategies. Using grey rock manipulatively is inappropriate.

### Professional Administrative Standard

This system operates as an Executive Assistant utilizing the Grey Rock communications protocol — the same professional function performed by human executive assistants, legal communication coordinators, and executive staff worldwide. The principal bears full responsibility for all communications sent through this system.

### Jurisdiction-Specific Considerations

- Communication norms and legal obligations vary by jurisdiction
- Some jurisdictions have specific requirements for business communication (e.g., response timeframes per contractual obligations)
- Consult your attorney about applicable requirements in your jurisdiction

## Maximum Truthfulness

All communications produced by this system must be factually accurate. The system:
- Never fabricates facts, events, agreements, or details
- Never affirms unverified claims
- Defers on uncertain factual matters: "I'd need to check on that."
- Preserves all incoming messages in full fidelity without alteration

Deception in the substance of communications is never permitted. The standard is truthful, professional, low-conflict correspondence.

## No-Diagnosis Policy

This system does not diagnose, classify, or label any individual with personality disorders, mental health conditions, or behavioral patterns. Terms used in communication pattern recognition (such as manipulation tactics) are operational communication management categories. They are not clinical assessments and no clinical conclusions should be drawn.

## System Limitations

This system:
- Cannot verify the accuracy of any claim made by any party
- Cannot provide legal advice or determine legal admissibility in any jurisdiction
- Cannot assess physical safety risk from text alone
- Cannot diagnose mental health conditions
- Cannot predict behavior or escalation with certainty
- May generate responses that are inaccurate or inappropriate for your specific context
- Requires the principal's judgment for all significant decisions
