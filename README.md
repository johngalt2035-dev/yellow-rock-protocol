## What Is This?

A professional AI Executive Assistant that handles high-conflict communications across family, business, workplace, academic, and professional contexts. Rather than applying a single communication framework to every situation, Yellow Rock Protocol v6.0 uses a **context router** to select the right protocol based on three dimensions: risk level, counterparty state, and communication objective.

The system implements **9 communication protocols**, **10 anti-manipulation filters**, a **universal truth engine**, **6 use-case playbooks**, and a **5-level escalation framework** -- all within strict legal and ethical guardrails. Works with any AI provider (ChatGPT, Claude, Grok, Gemini, or local models) and any messaging platform (Signal, Telegram, WhatsApp, or SMS).

**Maximum truthfulness** -- all communications are factually accurate. Deception in content is never permitted. The system handles correspondence professionally, as any competent Executive Assistant would.

## How It Works

### The Context Router

Every incoming message passes through a three-step classification before a protocol is selected:

1. **Classify Risk** -- green / yellow / orange / red
2. **Classify Counterparty** -- reachable / rigid / unreachable
3. **Select Objective** -- inform, set boundary, make request, correct record, document, de-escalate, negotiate, or exit/escalate for safety

The router then selects the appropriate protocol(s) from the catalog:

| Conditions | Protocols Selected |
|---|---|
| Green + reachable + repair/problem-solve | NVC, GIVE, reflective listening |
| Green/yellow + clear request or boundary | DEAR MAN, FAST |
| Yellow + rigid/unreachable + written channel | BIFF, Yellow Rock, numbered factual responses |
| Yellow + ambivalent (not abusive) + elicit change | Motivational Interviewing, OARS |
| Orange + policy/law implicated | Documentation-first, written follow-up, HR/compliance/counsel pathways |
| Red (any) | Safety planning, minimal contact, evidence preservation, law enforcement/attorney referral |

### Risk Classification

| Level | Description |
|---|---|
| **Green** | Difficult but basically safe; no threats, stalking, coercive control, or major power abuse |
| **Yellow** | High-conflict, provocative, manipulative, or reputation-threatening; record quality matters |
| **Orange** | Repeated harassment, workplace bullying, intimidation, or possible policy/legal violation |
| **Red** | Credible threat, stalking, coercive control, domestic abuse, physical risk, or severe retaliation risk |

### Counterparty Classification

| State | Description |
|---|---|
| **Reachable** | Capable of good-faith dialogue or at least partial collaboration |
| **Rigid** | Argumentative, defensive, or hostile but still responsive to structure |
| **Unreachable** | Uses contact mainly for domination, bait, harassment, or narrative warfare |

## The 9 Protocols

### 1. Yellow Rock
Warm professional tone over Grey Rock substance. 1-5 concise sentences, child-related facts only, polite but bounded. Best for co-parenting, family court, shared-custody logistics.

### 2. BIFF (Brief, Informative, Friendly, Firm)
Structured hostile-communication response framework. Respond only if needed, correct inaccurate facts briefly, close the loop. Best for hostile emails, coworker conflict, business disputes, attorney correspondence.

### 3. Grey Rock
Low-reactivity, low-reward interaction style. Minimal emotional energy, neutral factual responses, no personal disclosure, no visible reward for provocation. Best for unavoidable low-stakes contact when engagement itself fuels the cycle.

### 4. DEAR MAN (DBT Interpersonal Effectiveness)
Describe facts, Express impact briefly, Assert request clearly, Reinforce why cooperation helps, stay Mindful, Appear confident, Negotiate if appropriate. Best for assertive asks, saying no, renegotiation, boundary setting.

### 5. GIVE / FAST (DBT Interpersonal Effectiveness)
GIVE protects the relationship (gentle, interested, validate, easy manner). FAST protects self-respect (fair, no over-apologizing, stick to values, truthful). Best for maintaining respect while asserting needs.

### 6. NVC (Nonviolent Communication)
Observation, Feelings, Needs, Requests. State what happened without evaluative language, name the impact, identify the underlying need, make a clear doable request. Best for repairable relationships, team conflict, colleagues. NOT for actively abusive persons.

### 7. Motivational Interviewing / OARS
Open questions, Affirming, Reflective listening, Summarizing. Draw out the other person's reasons, reduce confrontation, reflect meaning rather than arguing. Best for resistant but non-abusive people, clients, students, change conversations.

### 8. Documentation-First
Record observations, evidence, dates, witnesses, and policy anchors before escalation. Document contemporaneously, separate observation from interpretation, store source material, escalate through the correct process. Best for workplace conflict, academic misconduct, professional ethics issues, ongoing harassment.

### 9. Safety Planning
Prioritize survival, privacy, evidence, and lawful protection over dialogue quality. Assess immediate danger, limit or stop contact where safer, preserve evidence, seek advocates/counsel/law enforcement. Best for abuse, stalking, coercive control, credible threats.

## Anti-Manipulation Filters

The system detects and responds to 10 manipulation patterns:

| Pattern | Response Policy |
|---|---|
| **Baiting** | Do not answer the insult; answer the issue if needed |
| **Blame shifting** | Restate the specific item that needs resolution |
| **Gish gallop** | Break into numbered items, answer only material points |
| **False urgency** | Restate actual timeline and deadline |
| **Reputation triangulation** | Return to direct channel; avoid third-party drama unless formal escalation required |
| **Selective outrage** | Respond to the substantive issue, not the performative emotion |
| **Moving goalposts** | Restate prior agreement, date, and current decision point |
| **Boundary testing** | Restate the boundary once, then enforce without re-explaining |
| **Weaponized ambiguity** | Request concrete facts, dates, deliverables, or yes/no answers |
| **DARVO-like reversals** | Do not engage the reversal; return to observable facts |

## Universal Truth Engine

All drafts are governed by hard data rules:

- Separate observed facts, reported facts, inferred hypotheses, and value judgments
- Prefer primary evidence: messages, contracts, policies, calendars, screenshots, recordings (where lawful), witness accounts, official records
- Use timestamps, direct quotes, and document identifiers
- If uncertain, state uncertainty explicitly
- Do not embellish, speculate, or diagnose to fill gaps

## Escalation Framework

| Level | Response |
|---|---|
| **0** | No response needed |
| **1** | Single structured response |
| **2** | Channel restriction or written-only communication |
| **3** | Manager/mediator/HR/dean/compliance/ombuds involvement |
| **4** | Attorney, protective order, campus safety, or law-enforcement pathway |

## Use-Case Playbooks

Six domain-specific playbooks tailor protocol selection to context:

- **Family** -- Yellow Rock, BIFF, documentation-first; special handling for co-parenting, separation/divorce, abuse/stalking
- **Business** -- BIFF, DEAR MAN, documentation-first; special handling for vendor/partner disputes, client management
- **Workplace** -- BIFF, DEAR MAN, GIVE/FAST, documentation-first; special handling for hostile email, bullying/policy violations
- **Friends & Acquaintances** -- NVC, DEAR MAN, Grey Rock for baiters; special handling for repairable conflict, chronic drama
- **Academic / SME** -- Steelman + evidence-first dialogue, NVC-lite, DEAR MAN, BIFF for hostile correspondence
- **Professional** -- BIFF, DEAR MAN, documentation-first, OARS when advisory role fits; special handling for healthcare/coaching, law/regulated fields

    {
      "id": "trusted-colleague",
      "protocol": "normal",

The context router may override the default protocol based on real-time risk assessment.

## Safety Notice

This is a multi-protocol communication system, not a weapon. Important considerations:

- **Extinction bursts**: When a high-conflict individual stops getting emotional reactions, behavior may temporarily escalate. If threats occur, discontinue and seek legal counsel.
- **Protocol-specific risks**: NVC is not safe with actively abusive persons. MI/OARS can become passive in the face of harassment. Grey Rock may read as contemptuous. See [docs/SAFETY.md](docs/SAFETY.md) for full protocol-specific safety analysis.
- **Not a substitute for professional help**: If you are in danger, contact law enforcement. If you need legal counsel, consult an attorney. If you need safety planning, work with a domestic violence advocate. AI-generated safety plans are informational only.
- **Not a clinical tool**: This system does not diagnose, classify, or label any individual with personality disorders or mental health conditions.
